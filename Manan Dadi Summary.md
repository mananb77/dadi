# **Memories with Dadi \- A Comprehensive Summary**

## **Her Presence & Essence**

### **Physical Memories**

* **Her appearance**: Sky blue was her favorite color \- the color of the sky. She wore it often throughout her life, in different celebrations and events. The photo used for her memorial website shows her in sky blue with flowers in her sari \- that's how you picture her.  
* **Her movements**: As she aged, her walk changed to more of a waddle, using a cane. You remember the sound of her footsteps, the way she moved through spaces.  
* **Her hands**: Soft but experienced \- hands that had rolled thousands of rotis, washed dishes, and done housework throughout her lifetime.  
* **Her scent**: Jasmine or rose \- the smell you associate with India.  
* **Her voice**: Present in the video recordings you have of her.

### **Her Personality**

* **Fierce determination**: She put 100% effort into everything she did. This wasn't just about being a grandmother \- she was a woman who fought hard to continue her education when it wasn't encouraged, who juggled MA and B.Ed after marriage and children.  
* **Action over words**: She showed love through actions, not explicit affection. She wasn't much of a hugger, didn't say "I love you" frequently, but expressed care through serving rotis, making food, and constant service.  
* **Selfless yet independent**: A fascinating contradiction \- she would serve everyone else first, eat last, do the dishes after everyone finished, yet fiercely insisted on doing everything herself to maintain her self-sufficiency and health.  
* **Strict about order**: She would get angry if something was out of place or not done properly.  
* **Playful with grandchildren**: She was lenient and playful about cards and making time for her grandkids \- that was always important to her.  
* **People-focused**: She thrived in big family environments and struggled with the quietness of weekdays in Sunnyvale when everyone was working. She was used to constant company in India.

## **Food & Kitchen Memories**

### **What She Made**

* **Roti sabji**: The main dish, made again and again. You can picture her hands rolling rotis on the belen, even on the ground in Jaipur.  
* **Saak (shakar bare)**: Her favorite thing to make.  
* **Namkeen and nashta**: Morning snacks that smelled amazing \- full of spices and different aromas.  
* **The cooking process**: She was always composed in the kitchen, knew how to move around, parallel process, manage multiple things at once.

### **Meal Rituals**

* **Her role at meals**: She would sit anywhere but would always be making rotis throughout the entire meal, continuously serving everyone at the table. She'd eat last, sometimes after everyone had finished.  
* **The importance of sitting together**: This was very important to her \- everyone eating together as a family.  
* **Chai times**: During chai time, there would be different snacks, bohre, nashta from the Indian market. On weekdays in Sunnyvale it was just your immediate family, but weekends were filled with people visiting.

### **Food Values She Taught**

* **Never waste food**: Not a single drop (boond) of dal, not a grain of rice, not any vegetables. She emphasized how food comes from farmers to the table, how lucky you are not to have to farm it yourselves.  
* **Share food**: Food was always made for everyone, meant to be shared. This created an environment of a shared home and family experience.

## **The Summer of 2024 \- Three Precious Months**

### **In Sunnyvale (May-July 2024\)**

**Daily rhythm**: You had a precious window from 5pm to 8:30pm each day to spend with her (after your work, before she went to bed).

**Activities together**:

* **Google Maps on the projector**: In the garage, showing her different places around the world. Her amazement: "I can't believe from here we can travel the world from sitting at home."  
* **Taking her to Michael's**: Getting yarn for knitting projects. It was wonderful for her to see arts and crafts and do creative work that wasn't a priority in Jaipur.  
* **She knitted items**: Including a sweater for Samya.

**Walking her through your education**:

* **Elementary school**: Showed her where you grew up, different classes, the playground, book fairs.  
* **Middle school**: Showed her the building from outside, talked about different subjects.  
* **High school**: Got to go inside, showed her robotics room, the Olympic pool where you swam for years, the large gymnasium, the amphitheater where you and friends hung out.  
* **UC Berkeley**: The most detailed tour \- shared stories of midterm exams, professors, clubs, activities, dorms, meal culture. You immersed her in your four-year experience there.

**Her reaction**: Pure pride. She was really happy and smiled seeing the college campus and pool. This was incredibly meaningful given her own struggles for education.

**The parallel moment**: In India, she had walked you through all her certificates \- BA in Economics, cookery/baking certificates. You have video of this. She wanted you to see how intelligent she was, how good she was at math. Then you walked her through your educational spaces. Both of you sharing your educational journeys with each other.

**Walking on Albatross Drive**: In 2024, she would make four rounds back and forth outside your house in the neighborhood because Ortega Park was too far. This was different from 2013 when she was younger and could walk to Ortega Park and back by herself.

**Pinecrest Lake trip (around July 4th)**:

* The family trip with drone show  
* **Stargazing moment**: You were all in the patio area. Her saying "Wow, there's so many stars in the sky."  
* This was one of her last trips to the US.

**Her wanting to return to India**: She kept saying she wanted to go back to India soon, as soon as possible. Your family convinced her to stay until your 21st birthday \- that was meaningful to have her there for that milestone. She stayed from May through July.

**Your 21st birthday**: Amazing celebration with both sides of the family coming together in San Ramon and Sunnyvale. She switched between staying with you and staying with Binu Chacha in San Ramon for a week, getting to spend time with both sons.

**The goodbye in late July**: She left with Nidhi auntie around July 25th. It was hard because you didn't know if you'd see her again. But you did \- you visited India in early 2025\.

**Your reflection on this time**: "I won't need to be living with regret" \- you consciously made time for her, carved out evenings and weekends even while working that summer. This is something you'll cherish forever.

## **Her Spiritual Life \- The Sanyas Ashram Stage**

### **Daily Practices in Her Later Years**

* **Writing "Jai Shri Krishna"**: Every single morning, two pages \= four malas. She filled entire notebooks with this practice. The notebook is back in India now. She was incredibly disciplined and consistent about this, both in Sunnyvale and in India. You can picture her sitting there writing those pages.  
* **Reading the Gita**: For knowledge about how to live life, how to understand different beings, nature, why things exist.  
* **Going to temple**: 7am daily for arti in Jaipur. Three different artis \- Shiv ji, Ram/Krishna/Lakshman/Sita/Hanuman/Radha Govind, and Ganesh ji. She'd do all three, take prasad, then come home.  
* **Attending religious events**: Listening to Shiv Puran and other teachings about creation, how to live life.

### **Her Philosophy**

She talked about the **four ashrams** (stages of life):

1. **Bal ashram** (0-25): Childhood/student life  
2. **Grihasth ashram** (25-50): Householder life  
3. **Vanprasth ashram** (50-75): Gradual withdrawal  
4. **Sanyas ashram** (75+): Spiritual focus, detachment

At 81, she was in sanyas ashram. This meant:

* Cutting ties with worldly things  
* Not wanting to attend marriages  
* Preferring to be alone to focus on her spiritual practices  
* Not wanting elaborate celebrations  
* Focusing on eventual moksha (liberation)

### **Her Beliefs**

* **Karma**: "If you give happiness, you'll receive happiness. If you give sorrow, you'll receive sorrow." After death, things come back 10 times stronger.  
* **Good deeds for good rebirth**: Satvik karma leads to dev yoni (divine birth), rajasik karma to human birth, tamasik karma to lower births.  
* **Don't create enemies**: Don't hate anyone. Every person is their own friend or enemy.  
* **Accept both good and bad**: Don't be overly happy or sad, treat everything equally.  
* **Let go of others' faults**: If you see someone's bad behavior, turn away and take your own path.

### **Your Reflection**

Her spiritual devotion kept her rooted and grounded. Her faith allowed her to live longer. These practices were really important parts of living long and being grounded and doing your part in the world.
