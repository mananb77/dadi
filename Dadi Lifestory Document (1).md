# **Dadi: A Life of Purpose, Love & Devotion (1944-2026)**

## **üë§ Who She Was**

Born in Delhi, 1944\. Seventh child in her family. Daughter of a railway mail guard (Senior Mail Guard who raised the flag at the station). A woman who broke barriers, pursued knowledge relentlessly, and dedicated 81 years to service, family, and unwavering faith. She lived in railway bungalows, walked kilometers to school with cloth bags, and grew up in an era without electricity or running water \- yet she never stopped reaching higher.

## **üìö The Scholar & Educator**

* **Education Pioneer**: Fought to continue her education when families didn't prioritize girls' schooling  
* **Government Girls Higher Secondary School, Dariyaganj, Delhi** \- High school  
* **Ramjas College, Morris Nagar, Delhi** \- B.A. Honours in Economics (studying in English, making notes in English, reading writers like Bentham and Malthus)  
* **Delhi School of Economics Library** \- Member, deepening her knowledge  
* **M.A. in Economics** (Meerut University ‚Üí Agra University after universities split)  
* **B.Ed. 1971** \- Became a mathematics teacher  
* **Teaching Career**: Taught Arithmetic, Geometry, and Algebra to 8th grade students. Known for teaching without looking at books \- everything from memory. Students' mothers still remember her fondly.

## **üéñÔ∏è The Warrior Spirit**

* **NCC (National Cadet Corps)**: Three years of service (First, Second, Third year of college)  
* **1962 Indo-China War Response**: Received rifle training, learned to shoulder a rifle and aim  
* **Daily Drill**: One hour of marching, formation exercises with sticks  
* **Philosophy Learned**: "Do and Die" \- a motto she carried throughout life  
* **Sports**: Played throwball/baseball, long jump, led exercise groups with staffs that had bells

## **üåè Life's Journey Across India**

**Delhi** (1944-1967) \- Childhood in Dariba Kalan, Old Delhi, near Red Fort and Chandni Chowk  
 **Jodhpur** (1967-1973) \- Sardarpura, B Road, Shiv Nivas \- where two sons were born  
 **Kota** (1973-1975) \- Shopping Center, Ghumanpura \- where third son was born  
 **Ajmer** (1975-1976) \- Raja Cycle area, near Martingal Bridge  
 **Udaipur** (1976-1981) \- Residency Road, Bhatt Ji Ki Bari, Bhopal Pura  
 **Jaipur** (1981-2025) \- 42 years in the same home, her longest and final residence

## **üí™ The Lifelong Learner**

She never stopped growing:

* **1959**: First Aid certification (St. John Ambulance, Delhi)  
* **1978**: Food Preservation (Udaipur) \- candy, jelly, jam, squashes, syrups  
* **1981**: Cookery & Bakery (Khasa Kothi, Jaipur) \- pizza, cakes, breads  
* **1987**: Cutting & Tailoring (6-month course) \- making clothes for her entire family  
* **1987**: Music (Second Year certification) \- Tabla and Harmonium from Prayag University  
* **2001**: Astrology (from Chennai)  
* Also: Kitchen Gardening Association member, won prizes for her garden

## **üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß‚Äçüë¶ Her Family & Values**

* **Married**: 1967, to a Union Bank of India officer who rose from Clerk to Manager  
* **Mother of 3 sons**: Raised them without tutors, teaching them herself until 12th grade  
* **Her Core Teaching**:  
  * "Never steal, never lie, never hurt anyone's tongue (heart) \- this is service to God"  
  * "Share everything you eat \- distribution, not hoarding"  
  * "If you find something, don't keep it. Return it or donate it to temple"  
  * "Work hard for what you want. Never take what isn't yours"  
* **Living Example**: When a son wanted to keep a pencil from school, she made him return it immediately  
* **Extended Family**: Took in and supported siblings-in-law, helped with their education, marriages, and jobs without ever complaining

## **üè† The Homemaker**

She believed her home was her temple:

* **Standard of Living**: Studied development of USA, Russia, Japan, England while doing her Economics degree. Applied those principles: "How do I develop my home? How do I raise my standard of living?"  
* **Choice**: Chose family over career \- "If I work, I'd have two jobs. Better to do one job perfectly."  
* **Cleanliness**: Obsessive about hygiene \- even dustbins had to be scrubbed clean. Washed buckets and mugs before every bath.  
* **Daily Routine**: Up early, 2-3 hours of house cleaning and organizing by noon, then spiritual practices

## **üïâÔ∏è Her Spiritual Journey**

* **Childhood Devotion**: At 9-10 years old, wrote 2,500 Gayatri mantras (certificate from 1960 still preserved)  
* **Service to Elders**: Read Krishna Leela and Ramayana to her illiterate grandmother  
* **Daily Practice in Final Years**:  
  * 7:00 AM temple visits for three aartis (Shiv ji, Ram-Krishna-Hanuman, Radha Govind, Ganesh ji)  
  * Writing "Jai Shri Krishna" 888 times daily (4 malas, 2 pages, 222 names per page)  
  * Reading Bhagavad Gita for life guidance  
  * Attending Shiv Puran recitations  
* **The Four Ashrams**: She lived consciously through each stage:  
  * **Bal Ashram (0-25)**: Learning and education  
  * **Grihastha Ashram (25-50)**: Family and responsibilities  
  * **Vanprastha Ashram (50-75)**: Guidance and stepping back  
  * **Sanyas Ashram (75+)**: Devotion and detachment \- "Cut ties slowly, focus on God"

## **üíô Her Essence**

**Favorite Color**: Sky blue \- "Aasmani"  
 **Favorite Animal**: Parrots (kept them at home, fed cows daily bread)  
 **Favorite Food**: All vegetables \- "Each has different qualities, I eat them all"  
 **Health Conscious**:

* Learned from childhood health lectures: "Wash mouth and eyes with water held in cheeks" \- kept her eyes perfect at 81  
* Strong teeth from chewing raw sugarcane (ganna) her whole life  
* After hospitalization (blood sugar dropped to 31), followed doctor's orders religiously: eat every 2 hours

## **üåü Her Philosophy**

From the Bhagavad Gita, she believed:

* **Karma**: "Give happiness, receive happiness. Give sorrow, receive sorrow 10-fold"  
* **Yogini (Rebirth)**: Do good deeds (Satvik karma) ‚Üí get Dev yoni (godly birth). Do average deeds (Rajas karma) ‚Üí get human birth. Do bad deeds (Tamas karma) ‚Üí get lower births  
* **No Enemies**: "Every person is their own friend or enemy. Don't create enemies, create friends"  
* **Let Go of Bad**: "If you see someone's bad qualities, turn away and walk your own path. Don't hold hatred"  
* **Equanimity**: "Don't be too happy or too sad. Treat everything equally and move forward with good things"

## **üíî Final Chapter**

In her final years, she had completed her duties:

* All three sons educated (one to IIT), married, settled  
* Grandchildren thriving  
* She had transitioned fully into Sanyas Ashram  
* "I don't want moksha (liberation) immediately. Do good deeds, enjoy heaven for a while, then come back to serve again"  
* She survived a critical health scare (blood sugar 31\) because "everyone's prayers worked"  
* She lived simply, prayed daily, and found peace in routine and devotion

## **üìñ Her Legacy**

She didn't pursue a PhD or a big career, but she created something greater: three educated sons, a loving family spanning four generations, and a living example of how to walk through life with integrity, devotion, and grace.

Her students remember her. Their mothers still speak of her. Her children embody her values. Her grandchildren recorded her stories. Her great-grandchildren will know her voice.
